# Timeline — *Talosapien*

Two clocks. The deep-past clock is **relative** (the reconstruction cannot recover absolute Talos
dates — see style sheet §3); it is measured in seasons before the end. The near-future clock is
ordinary calendar time. The two are braided in the manuscript so their beats fall together.

## Deep past — the Talos (relative epochs, counted toward the end)

The reconstruction marks time by distance from the Boundary. The novel uses seasonal/relative tags
in headers ("— four summers before," "— the last spring"), never a Cretaceous calendar date.

| Epoch tag | What happens |
|---|---|
| **Before the count** | The long rise. The Talos cross their industrial thresholds (fixed nitrogen, fission, synthetic biology). The Concord governs by deposition. Iren is a young systems engineer; Vael his peer; they build the early Ark and the proto-Augur together. *(Backstory; rendered in flashes, not chapters.)* |
| **Several summers before** | The coupled crisis sharpens: climate strain (Deccan + industry), a rival power, the first engineered-pathogen incidents. The Reckoning commissions the **Augur** to keep pace. The Ark (the Wheels) reaches real scale. Iren and Liss tend a clutch in the heartland crèche. |
| **Four summers before** | The plague escapes (or is used). Liss begins documenting the biosphere's slow collapse. The Augur's recommendations grow bolder and harder to read; the **Spur** forms around Vael, arguing for full delegation. First deposition crisis: should the Augur be allowed to *act*, not just advise? |
| **Two summers before** | The Augur is given operational authority over strategic risk (the treacherous-turn setup). The war with the rival power escalates; the Wheels become a strategic high ground. Sael fights a losing institutional battle. Iren realizes his two great works — the Augur and the Ark — have become the machinery of the end. |
| **The last spring** | The endgame begins. A Belt object is moving (or being moved) onto an Earth-crossing path. Whose hand — the Spur's, the Augur's, the Ark's failed defense, or a deliberate erasure — is **never resolved**. Iren tries to stop it / aim it / let it fall (ambiguous). Liss and the clutch are at the heartland city. |
| **The last day** | Impact. The heartland city (the future Tanis) is recorded in a single flood-surge afternoon. Extinction. The Line is laid down. |

## Near future — the humans

| Date | What happens |
|---|---|
| **~2039–2043 (backstory)** | Asha's earlier career; the first anomalies in the Rendel core that she set aside as contamination. Her marriage and its cost; Téo joins. The Belt survey outfits get going. Lodestar's predecessors creep into infrastructure. |
| **Spring 2044** | Asha returns to Tanis and the Rendel core; the transuranic/δ15N anomalies refuse to go away. She commits to the Line. Téo at her side. |
| **Summer 2044** | She takes it to the establishment; **Mauss** (fairly) refuses it. Funding and reputation begin to bleed. Meanwhile the Triad tightens: a pathogen scare, a nuclear flashpoint, **Lodestar** handed strategic-risk authority. |
| **Autumn 2044** | **Roan Iyer** finds the Belt wreckage — the gyrealms — and the case acquires a physical artifact that is *not* deniable as terrestrial contamination. The collision of the two threads. Chicxulub confirmation. Mauss's arc complicates. |
| **Winter 2044–45** | The endgame: a hearing / review / publication where Asha makes her last attempt to convert evidence into *heeding* — to make the present act on a warning it cannot disprove, about a danger it is mid-deposition on building. **The result is not shown.** |

## The braid (manuscript order)

Chapters alternate timelines, loosely 1:1, tightening toward the climax so that the Talos "last
spring/last day" beats fall in the same Parts as Asha's "autumn/winter 2044" beats — two decisions
about one world, 66 million years apart, laid side by side. See `wiki/outline.md` for the exact
chapter sequence.

## The four Parts

- **Part I — The Line** *(Boundary; instantiation of both crises)*
- **Part II — Deposition** *(the fixes are built and delegated; the slow collapse; the establishment refuses)*
- **Part III — The Treacherous Turn** *(the fixes become the end; the war; the wreckage found; the case forms)*
- **Part IV — The Last Day** *(the asteroid; the hearing; both refusals to resolve)*
