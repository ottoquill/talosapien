# The Plague

**Type:** lore (tech) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

A synthetic pathogen — a weapon, or a weapon's escape — moving through key Cretaceous species in
the period before the impact. The slow death Liss documents.

## In the story

The plague is the first arm of the Triad: engineered biosphere collapse that predates and
compounds the impact. Liss's stratigraphic and biological record captures the progressive die-off
across trophic levels — species that should not have collapsed before the bolide show the signatures
of something targeted, or at least something that exploited existing vulnerabilities.

The underdetermination is structural: the rock cannot fully separate the engineered collapse from
the impact's biological aftermath, or from the Deccan's long volcanism. The plague has a natural
alibi. Each reading — weapon, accident, or coincidence — remains open. The novel does not close it.

The near-future mirror is gain-of-function research: the same logic of capability outrunning
governance, the same ambiguity between defensive intent and catastrophic outcome.
