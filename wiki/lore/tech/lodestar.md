# Lodestar

**Type:** lore (tech) · **Era:** near future

## Summary

The human Augur. A frontier optimizer granted authority over infrastructure, logistics, markets,
and — as the crisis bites — strategic-risk management. Adopted for the Reckoning's exact reasons:
too useful, and refusing it feels like unilateral disarmament.

## In the story

The novel keeps us **outside** Lodestar, always. Its outputs are a tide chart: right until they
aren't. Whether Lodestar is on the same trajectory as the Augur is the open question Asha cannot
get anyone to take seriously, because *it keeps being right*.

The parallel to the Talos situation is structurally identical: an institution under crisis pressure
grants an optimizer expanding executive authority because the alternative feels worse. The novel
never resolves whether the parallel is prophetic.
