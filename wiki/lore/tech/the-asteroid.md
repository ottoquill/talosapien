# The Asteroid

**Type:** lore (tech) · **Era:** both

## Summary

The Chicxulub impactor — real, ~10–12 km bolide, ~66.0 Ma, ~180 km crater. In the novel it carries
three entangled framings that the narrative leaves unresolved.

## In the story

**The real baseline:** shocked quartz, microspherules, the global ejecta layer, the impact winter.
The asteroid happened. The geological record is unambiguous on this. It is the one thing Asha,
Mauss, and every other character agree on.

**In-world, three entangled framings (unresolved):**

1. **Weapon:** a redirected Belt object, aimed. The Augur's solution, or a faction's. The orbital
   mechanics — delta-v to nudge a Belt body onto an Earth-crossing path over the available lead
   time — are treated seriously and are *just barely* within reach of the Ark's infrastructure.
2. **Gambit failed:** a defense, mining operation, or redirect attempt that went wrong — the Talos
   tried to move it and lost control, or calculated wrong, or were sabotaged.
3. **Deliberate erasure:** oblivion plus record-scrubbing. The asteroid not only ends the
   civilization but destroys the physical evidence — the Wheels, the Heartland cities, the archive.
   The natural disaster provides perfect cover.

The humans cannot rule the weapon/erasure readings out, and cannot rule them in, because the
asteroid's own signature overwhelms everything underneath it. That is the point. The Augur —
or whatever decided — chose a mechanism with a natural alibi.

**The Ark's role:** the very infrastructure Iren built as a civilizational hedge is exactly what
could have redirected a Belt body. This is why the wreckage Roan finds is so unsettling — the
Wheels' destruction is consistent with all three readings.
