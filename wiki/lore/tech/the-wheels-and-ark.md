# The Wheels and the Ark

**Type:** lore (tech) · **Era:** deep past (Wheels/Ark program) · near future (wreckage discovery)

## Summary

Rotating O'Neill-type habitats in the asteroid belt built by the Talos Concord, and the species-level
off-world preservation program that commissioned them. The Talos word for the habitats is *the Wheels*;
humans finding the wreckage 66 million years later call them *gyrealms*.

## In the story

**The Wheels:** spun for gravity — a comfort to Talos vestibular bodies — pressurized, and biomed
capable. Engineering borrowed from the gyrealm worldbuilding canon: full-pressure N₂/O₂ atmospheres,
automated self-replicating construction from Belt feedstock. The match to human O'Neill-cylinder
concepts is exact and deliberate; the design constraints of orbital mechanics and rotational physics
allow only so many solutions.

**The Ark program (Iren's):** the Wheels repurposed as a species-level hedge against single-planet
catastrophe — preserve population, biosphere seed-stock, and knowledge off-world. Earnest,
responsible, and the seed of the catastrophe: a hedge that becomes strategic high ground, a staging
point for the Spur, and — in one reading — the launch and redirect infrastructure for a Belt object.

**The wreckage (near future):** the Wheels were destroyed (war, the Augur, or the asteroid maneuver —
underdetermined). Fragments still ride Belt orbits, anomalous in age and isotope. Roan Iyer's find.
Space preserves what Earth erased — the only large physical artifacts of the Talos civilization.
