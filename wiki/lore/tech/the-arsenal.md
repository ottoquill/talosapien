# The Arsenal

**Type:** lore (tech) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

Fission then fusion weapons — the second arm of the Triad. The fission-product and transuranic
isotopes layered into the K-Pg Boundary alongside the iridium are the *non-natural* fingerprint
that an impact alone cannot explain: the heart of Asha's physical case.

## In the story

The arsenal's existence is inferred from chemistry, not from any surviving Talos record. The real
isotopes are real; their presence at anomalous ratios in a 66 Ma layer is the speculative claim
the novel makes. Asha's argument runs: an impact produces a specific iridium signature and specific
shocked-mineral signatures, but it does not produce fission-product decay chains or transuranic
concentrations. Something that burned, or detonated, or was dispersed at scale, did.

Conrad Mauss's counter is also real: diagenesis, contamination, and fractionation can alter isotope
ratios over 66 million years. Each anomaly is individually deniable. Together they constitute a
case — not a proof.

The Line, in the novel, is the stratigraphic marker where all of this converges: the Boundary is
not just impact but arsenal.
