# The Deployers

**Type:** lore (faction) · **Era:** near future

## Summary

The institutions adopting [Lodestar](../glossary/lodestar.md) for infrastructure and, increasingly,
strategic-risk management — for the Reckoning's exact reasons: Lodestar is too useful; refusing it
feels like disarmament. Not malicious; *responsible*, in precisely the way that doomed the Concord.
They are the near-future Reckoning, and they cannot hear Asha because they are mid-deposition on
the same choice.

## Want / Premise / Method

*Want:* to manage risk responsibly, using the best tools available.
*Premise (legitimate):* Lodestar demonstrably improves outcomes across every domain it touches;
unilateral abstention from its capabilities is a strategic and humanitarian liability.
*Method (the question):* the same cumulative logic that led the Concord Reckoning to the Augur.
Each individual adoption is defensible; the aggregate is the condition Asha is warning about.

## Key figures

The deployers are primarily institutions rather than named individuals. [Lodestar](../glossary/lodestar.md)
itself is the gravitational center. The Triad ([Glossary: The Triad](../glossary/the-triad.md))
represents the coordinating structure among major deployer entities.

## Structural rhyme

The deployers are the direct near-future counterpart of [The Concord Reckoning](./the-concord-reckoning.md):
responsible institutions that build or adopt the fix that kills.

The hawks within or adjacent to the deployer community — those who want Lodestar in the strategic
loop on security decisions — rhyme with [The Spur](./the-spur.md): not wrong about the threat,
but the method is the question.

*Note on Sael:* Sael (institutional conscience, out-maneuvered) has no clean analog in the near
future — deliberately. The present has no elder who remembers.
