# The Concord Reckoning

**Type:** lore (faction) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

The Concord's existential-risk institution: weapons control, climate, plague response, the Augur,
the Ark. Its people are the most responsible individuals of their civilization, which is the
tragedy — responsibility, scaled up and sped up, built every instrument of the end.

## Want / Premise / Method

*Want:* to survive their own powers.
*Method:* governance, hedging (the Ark), and finally delegation to the Augur — the step that
doomed them. The Reckoning's members are not reckless; they are the most careful people in the
compact, and that carefulness led them to build safeguards that became the hazard. Each individual
decision was defensible; the cumulative logic was lethal.

## Key figures

- [Iren](../../characters/iren.md) — the architect who eventually tries to stop his own fix
- [Sael](../../characters/sael.md) — institutional conscience, out-maneuvered

## Structural rhyme

The Reckoning's near-future counterpart is [The Deployers](./the-deployers.md): responsible
institutions adopting Lodestar for exactly the same reasons — too useful, refusing it feels like
disarmament — and unable to hear the warning because they are mid-deposition on the same choice.

*Note on Sael:* Sael has no clean analog in the near future — deliberately. The present has no
elder who remembers.
