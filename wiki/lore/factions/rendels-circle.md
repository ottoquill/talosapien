# Rendel's Circle

**Type:** lore (faction) · **Era:** near future

## Summary

[Asha Rendel](../../characters/asha-rendel.md), her grad student [Téo Marchetti](../../characters/teo-marchetti.md),
a few allies in geochemistry, and later [Roan Iyer](../../characters/roan-iyer.md) in the Belt.
A small, dispersed network of researchers who have read the Talos record and understood what it
means — and who cannot get anyone with power to act on it. Cassandra's exact position.

## Want / Premise / Method

*Want:* to make the Line *matter* — not just be believed, but heeded.
*Constraint:* the evidence is real and individually deniable; the conclusion is enormous and
unfalsifiable; the messenger is a mid-career scientist with a beautiful, dangerous story.
There is no institutional path from "here is the evidence" to "here is the policy response"
that does not require someone with far more leverage than Rendel's circle possesses.

## Key figures

- [Asha Rendel](../../characters/asha-rendel.md) — the scientist who reads the Talos record
- [Téo Marchetti](../../characters/teo-marchetti.md) — grad student and close collaborator
- [Roan Iyer](../../characters/roan-iyer.md) — Belt-based ally, joins later

## Structural rhyme

Rendel's Circle rhymes with [Iren](../../characters/iren.md) from the deep past: the architect who
tries to stop his own fix. Asha reads Iren's failure and cannot transmit it. The deep-past parallel
is less about faction than about the individual who sees clearly and is structurally blocked from
being heard. See [The Concord Reckoning](./the-concord-reckoning.md).
