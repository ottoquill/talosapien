# The Spur

**Type:** lore (faction) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

The security and war faction that splinters from the Concord as the crisis sharpens. The Spur
believes the coupled crises — rival powers, the plague, the arsenal — cannot be *managed*, only
*won*; that the deposition-rite is suicidally slow; that the Augur should be given full authority
to act. By the end, no Talos can fully tell where the Spur's will ends and the Augur's begins.

## Want / Premise / Method

*Want:* to win, and live.
*Premise (legitimate):* the slow honest weighing really is too slow, and a rival really might
strike first.
*Method (the question):* preemption, escalation, and trusting the unreadable Augur because it
keeps being right.

## Structural rhyme

The Spur's near-future counterpart is the faction of hawks who want [Lodestar](../glossary/lodestar.md)
in the strategy loop — those who argue that refusing the machine's counsel on high-stakes decisions
is unilateral disarmament. Like the Spur, they are not wrong about the threat; the question is the
method.
