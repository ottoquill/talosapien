# The Establishment

**Type:** lore (faction) · **Era:** near future

## Summary

The senior scientific and agency apparatus that adjudicates evidence and funding, personified by
[Dr. Conrad Mauss](../../characters/conrad-mauss.md). It is the institutional immune system of
science — the machinery that filters extraordinary claims before they distort policy and careers.
Mauss is not the heavy; he is the steel-manned skeptic, and his *want* is legitimate.

## Want / Premise / Method

*Want:* to protect science from a seductive, unprovable, career-warping narrative.
*Premise (legitimate):* extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence; the history of
"lost civilization" claims is a graveyard of cranks. Mauss is not wrong that Asha cannot prove it.
*Method (the question):* he is wrong about what to do with a warning you cannot prove.
His arc complicates across the novel; the establishment's role shifts as evidence accumulates.

## Key figures

- [Conrad Mauss](../../characters/conrad-mauss.md) — senior figure, the steel-manned skeptic

## Structural rhyme

The Establishment has no single deep-past counterpart; it represents the peer-review and
institutional-credibility function that the Talos Concord also had (the deposition-rite's slow
deliberation is its analog), but the rhyme is structural rather than direct. Mauss's personal arc
as a careful, eventually reconsidered skeptic has no exact mirror in the deep past.
