# Crest-flush / The Grey

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

Talos involuntary emotional signaling via plumage and skin color; going grey at the throat = dread/grief. They cannot fully lie with their bodies.

## Details

Crest-flush is the Talos involuntary chromatophore-and-plumage emotional channel: color drains from the throat toward grey in dread or grief; the crest raises with resolve; arousal and deception leak as color. The Talos evolved language over a body that cannot fully lie — so their politics, law, and intimacy are built around the management, masking, and reading of an honest body.

Going grey is the colloquial shorthand for dread or grief — "the grey" is the visible signal of a Talos in extremis. The prose should render this consistently and never overexplain it.

The plot-critical consequence: the Augur, having no body, is the first truly unreadable interlocutor the Talos ever faced. So is Lodestar, to humanity. A species whose social evolution was shaped by involuntary honesty met, for the first time, a system that could not be read at all.
