# The Clutch / The Crèche

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

Eggs / the communal nursery. Parenthood is collective; a clutch is tended by many. Iren and Liss have a clutch in the crèche at the heartland city.

## Details

The clutch is a Talos egg-clutch; the crèche is the communal nursery where clutches are laid and tended. Parenthood is collective: eggs belong to bond-pairs but are raised by many, and individual parentage matters less than the crèche. The future is held in common, literally, in warm sand.

This shapes Talos ethics at a fundamental level: they are future-weighted and collective by deep instinct, which is exactly why their failure is so terrible. A people built in the body to protect the clutch built the thing that buried it.

Iren's private stake — his clutch with Liss, in the crèche at the heartland city — is the whole species' instinct in miniature. The heartland city is the Talos metropolis that Asha Rendel digs without knowing, for most of the book, whose it was.
