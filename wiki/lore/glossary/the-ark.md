# The Ark

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

Iren's program: the Wheels as a hedge against single-planet catastrophe; preserve the species and its seed-stock off-world. Becomes a strategic target and staging ground.

## Details

The Ark is the program — Iren's — that built out the Wheels in the Belt as an insurance policy for Talos civilization: if catastrophe struck Earth, the species and its biological seed-stock would survive off-world. The Wheels were built for the Ark's purposes: rotating habitats in the asteroid belt, spun for gravity, pressurized, biome'd, stocked with life.

The Ark's logic was sound. Its failure was strategic: the Wheels, once built, became a high-ground asset the Spur could stage from. The hedge became a target and then a weapon. In one of the three readings of the ending, the Wheels become the launch infrastructure for redirecting a Belt object at Earth.

The Ark is one of the deepest structural ironies of the novel: the most responsible act of Talos civilization — hedging against extinction — contributed to extinction. Iren carries this.
