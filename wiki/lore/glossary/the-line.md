# The Line

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** frame / near future

## Summary

The iridium anomaly at the K–Pg boundary; colloquially, among Asha's circle, the whole body of evidence for *Talos sapiens*. "Reading the Line."

## Details

In strict geological usage, the Line is the global iridium anomaly first documented by the Alvarez team in 1980 — the centimeter or less of boundary clay that marks 66 Ma. In the novel's near-future usage, Asha's circle extends the term to mean the complete evidentiary corpus that makes the case for the Talos: not just the iridium but the anomalous transuranic and fission-product isotopes that a bolide impact alone cannot account for, the industrial δ15N and δ13C excursions, the trace-metal layering, and the microstructure in shocked spherules. Reading the Line means reading all of it together, and accepting what it implies.

The Line is the evidence; the Reconstruction is what you build from it. Asha's life work is arguing that the two are inseparable — that once you have read the Line honestly, the Reconstruction is not optional speculation but a forced conclusion. Conrad Mauss disagrees.
