# The Wheels

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

The Talos word (rendered) for their rotating Belt habitats. The humans, finding the wreckage, call them **gyrealms** (their own coinage for their own future habitats) before they understand what they are looking at.

## Details

The Wheels are rotating O'Neill-type habitats built in the asteroid belt, where raw material is abundant and a catastrophe on Earth would not reach. Engineered identically in principle to the human gyrealm concept: spun for artificial gravity, pressurized, biome'd. The Talos vestibular system — exquisite sense of rotation inherited from their agile ancestors — means the spin of the Wheels felt uncannily right to them, a comfort humans would not share in the same way.

The Wheels were the physical expression of the Ark program: Iren's hedge against single-planet catastrophe. They became instead a strategic high ground that the Spur could stage from — and, in one reading of the ending, the launch infrastructure for redirecting a Belt object at Earth.

Their wreckage still rides Belt orbits 66 million years on: anomalous, ancient, engineered debris with isotope signatures no natural body carries. This is what Roan Iyer finds. The humans called them gyrealms before they understood. The Wheels are the largest surviving physical artifacts of Talos civilization.
