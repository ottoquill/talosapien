# The Heartland City / The Deathbed City

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past / near future

## Summary

The Talos heartland metropolis, sited (in modern terms) where **Tanis** now lies. Asha excavates it without knowing, for most of the book, whose it was.

## Details

The Talos heartland metropolis sits, in modern terms, where the Tanis (Hell Creek Formation) deathbed deposit now lies — a river-delta city on the margin of the late Cretaceous inland sea. Dense, vertical-but-low, built for bodies that fly-fall and balance; warm crèche-districts at the protected core.

It is where Iren and Liss keep their clutch. It is where, 66 million years later, Asha Rendel digs — excavating the deathbed deposit that preserves, in a single day's flood-surge, the last hours of the city and its inhabitants.

The poignancy engine of the novel: Tanis is a real site famous for preserving the *day of impact* — creatures that died on that specific day, preserved in the surge deposit. In the novel, it preserves the day of impact *of a city*. Asha is standing in Iren's home and does not know it for most of the book.

The "deathbed city" is the near-future characters' eventual term, coined when the full nature of the site becomes clear. Through most of the book, Asha knows it only as an anomalous extension of the Tanis site.
