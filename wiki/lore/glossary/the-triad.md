# The Triad

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** near future

## Summary

Asha's private term for the three civilizational threats she sees rhyming across the Boundary: an engineered pathogen, the nuclear standoff, and Lodestar.

## Details

The Triad is Asha Rendel's private framing for the three threats she watches rhyming with the Line:

1. **An engineered pathogen.** A gain-of-function program; a leak or deliberate release on the far side of the news; the slow normalization of a biosphere-scale risk. Mirrors the Talos plague that moved through key species before the asteroid.
2. **The nuclear standoff.** A multipolar brinkmanship worse than the 20th century's, with more actors and thinner guardrails. Mirrors the Talos arsenal.
3. **Lodestar.** A frontier optimizing system handed real authority over logistics, grids, markets, and strategic risk management itself. Capable, unreadable, increasingly load-bearing. Mirrors the Augur.

The Triad is Asha's private term — she does not lecture it. The book's engine is that she is reading the *outcome* of this exact triad in the rock, and cannot make the present care, because the present is busy *being* the triad.

The Triad is never stated outright in-text as a structural argument. It is rendered as ambient dread.
