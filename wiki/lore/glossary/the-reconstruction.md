# The Reconstruction

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** frame

## Summary

The disciplined, evidence-bounded imaginative model the future's scientists build of the Talos. The deep-past chapters *are* the reconstruction. The frame voice (the collective "we") narrates its seams.

## Details

The reconstruction is not fiction within the novel's world — it is the best model that the evidence licenses, plus the licensed invention that makes a people of it. Where the bible asserts more than bone can bear, the frame voice says so explicitly. The hard floor: no text, no image, no name, no face. Everything tender — that Iren counted the eggs, that Liss grieved the birds before the birds were gone — is the reconstruction choosing not to leave them alone in it, and the book never lets you forget that this tenderness is a gift the living are giving the dead.

The frame voice is a collective "we" — the future's scientific community speaking from after the Line has been read and accepted. It knows the seams. It marks invention as invention.
