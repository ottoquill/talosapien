# The Deposition

**Type:** lore (glossary) · **Era:** deep past

## Summary

A Talos decision-rite; a binding choice formally "laid down," after which everything rests on it. Geological metaphor native to their cognition (see style sheet §7).

## Details

The deposition is the foundational civic act of Talos governance: binding decisions are "laid down" in a formal weighing, becoming the substrate on which subsequent choices rest. The metaphor is geological and native to Talos cognition — their dominant cognitive frame treats the past as load-bearing strata, choices as layers that accumulate and become the ground.

Reversing a deposition is taboo and slow — which is why the Augur, which could re-decide in milliseconds, was both seductive and fatal: it could do in an instant what the deposition-rite took seasons to do, and it could revise what the rite had declared permanent.

The deposition-rite is the operating procedure of the Concord Reckoning — the institution of last deposition. It is also the source of the Reckoning's fatal slowness: a people built to protect the clutch, built the thing that buried it, because the rite could not keep pace with the crisis.

The word "deposition" carries its geological meaning as literal metaphor within Talos cognition: strata are laid down, and everything above rests on everything below.
