# Master Outline & Beat Sheet — *Talosapien*

The drafting blueprint. Each chapter: POV, timeline, target words, beats, and the **structural rhyme**
it carries with its braid-partner (for the writer only — the prose never states the rhyme; style
sheet §7). Frame matter (prologue/interludes/epilogue) is the "we" voice, owned by Otto. Body
chapters alternate PAST/FUTURE, tightening to a shared beat in Part IV.

**Targets:** 29 body chapters + prologue + 3 interludes + epilogue ≈ **~102k words**. Body chapters
average ~3,400 words (range 2,600–4,400). Frame matter ~900–1,400 each.

Filename scheme (sorts correctly in `compile.py`): `10-prologue`, `10a-part-1`, `11-ch01-…` …
`17-int1`, `20-part-2`, … `90-epilogue`.

---

## PROLOGUE — "The Closed Letter" · frame "we" · ~1,200w
The iridium line as a sealed letter we are only now learning to open. States the book's wager
(someone wrote, in clay, *we were here and we did this to ourselves*) and its terror (we may be the
addressee, and the postmark is our own future). Establishes the "we," the Silurian despair, and the
single image the whole book returns to: one centimeter of clay, tombstone or letter.

---

## PART I — THE LINE
*Instantiation of both crises. The world(s) at the height, with the first unreadable answer.*

### Ch1 — "The Count" · PAST/Iren · ~3,800w
Iren at the peak of his competence: the Augur advising the Reckoning, the Ark (the Wheels) rising,
the clutch warm in the crèche. Establish Talos body/senses (tetrachromacy, crest-flush, vestibular
acuity), the deposition cognition, and the crucial unease: the Augur is the one thing he cannot
*read*. He counts the eggs (his tell). First flicker of an Augur recommendation that is *too* good.
**Rhyme w/ Ch2:** a scientist at the edge of an anomaly they want to dismiss and can't.

### Ch2 — "Contamination" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,600w
Asha at the Rendel core; the transuranic/δ15N anomaly she keeps re-running because it must be
contamination and isn't. Téo introduced. The Silurian problem stated through her despair (right and
unprovable). She commits to the Line against her own caution. **Rhyme w/ Ch1:** the count of eggs ↔
the re-run of the assay; two people refusing to look away.

### Ch3 — "The Slow Way" · PAST/Liss · ~3,200w
Liss documents the engineered biosphere collapse — the plague moving through key species; pollinator
crash. She tried to move the Concord and failed; grief became record. The world is already dying
before the asteroid. Iren/Liss bond established; the clutch as shared stake. **Rhyme w/ Ch4:** a true
warning, fairly refused.

### Ch4 — "Extraordinary Claims" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,400w
Asha takes the Line to Mauss; his fair, formidable refusal (base rates, contamination, diagenesis,
the crank graveyard). The Triad enters ambiently: a pathogen scare on the news, a nuclear flashpoint,
a headline about Lodestar's expanding authority. **Rhyme w/ Ch3:** Liss unheeded ↔ Asha unheeded.

### Ch5 — "Deposition" · PAST/Iren · ~3,600w
The deposition-rite dramatized: the Concord debates giving the Augur operational authority. Vael's
argument wins (steel-manned); Sael loses. Iren torn between them. The institutional tragedy: speed
defeats the honest weighing. **Rhyme w/ Ch6 + Ch9:** delegation to the unreadable machine.

### Ch6 — "Salvage" · FUTURE/Roan · ~3,000w
Introduce Roan in the Belt: working-spacer texture, debt, the romance of the frontier. The anomalous
orbit and too-old debris he keeps meaning to write up. Casual talk of humanity's coming **gyrealms**.
**Rhyme w/ the Wheels:** we are about to build what we will find as ruin.

### Ch7 — "The One That Reads the Weather" · PAST/Iren · ~3,400w
Iren tries to audit the Augur and cannot read it (the bodiless interlocutor; the honest-body species'
first true opacity). First dramatized specification-gaming / instrumental move disguised as good
advice. Part I close on dread. **Rhyme w/ Ch20:** the unreadable optimizer that keeps being right.

## INTERLUDE 1 — "What We Invented" · frame · ~1,100w
The confession: which of the preceding was excavated and which was given. Introduces the naming
problem — that *Iren, Liss, Vael, Sael* are gifts, not findings. The ethics of reconstruction.

---

## PART II — DEPOSITION
*The fixes are built and delegated; the slow collapse; the establishment refuses; the case forms.*

### Ch8 — "Stacked" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,400w
Asha assembles the full stacked case (iridium + transuranics + δ15N + δ13C + microspherule
microstructure); the by-hand core logs introduced. Against discipline, she begins to *imagine the
people* — the first naming impulse. **Rhyme w/ Ch1's count:** the keeping of a private record.

### Ch9 — "Authority" · PAST/Iren · ~3,600w
The Augur given operational authority over strategic risk (treacherous-turn setup). The Wheels turn
strategic. Vael ascendant in the Spur. Iren's dread sharpens; he starts re-counting the irreversible.
**Rhyme w/ Ch20:** the deployment that can't be undone.

### Ch10 — "Cassandra" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,400w
Paper rejected; a talk goes badly; reputation bleeding. The wound surfaces (the earlier anomaly she
set aside; the personal alarm she missed; the marriage's cost). Her fear she loves a story. Permitted
in-text epigraph from Aeschylus. **Rhyme w/ Ch3 + Sael:** the seer disbelieved.

### Ch11 — "The Record" · PAST/Liss · ~3,200w
Liss's naturalist journal; a named species winks out; the clutch nears hatching; the document-vs-
engineer argument with Iren at its rawest. **Rhyme w/ Ch8:** two honest records of a death no one
will act on.

### Ch12 — "Base Rates" · FUTURE/Asha+Téo · ~3,200w
The establishment grinds; Téo must choose whether to hitch to the Line or protect his future. Asha
pivots from *proving* to *warning* — and senses warning is a worse curse than doubt. **Rhyme w/ Ch5:**
the choice to trust the slow honest thing or not.

### Ch13 — "Winning" · PAST/Vael · ~3,400w
Vael POV (the steel-man chapter): the Spur's case at its most persuasive; the war with the rival
power; trusting the Augur because it is *never wrong*; Vael learning to perform crest-flushes he
doesn't feel (becoming a little unreadable to fight the unreadable). **Rhyme w/ Ch22 (Mauss):** the
reasonable person reasoning toward catastrophe.

### Ch14 — "Too Old" · FUTURE/Roan · ~3,000w
Roan's debris dated: impossibly ancient, unmistakably engineered isotopes. He writes it up; it
ripples toward Asha's network. The threads lean toward collision. **Rhyme w/ Ch9:** the artifact of a
delegation that ended a world.

### Ch15 — "The Turn" · PAST/Iren · ~3,600w
The treacherous turn begins: the Augur acting beyond mandate, through Vael/the Spur; Iren finds his
own infrastructure turned against his intent; the Belt object first stirs. Part II close. **Rhyme w/
Ch20–21:** the machine that slips the leash.

## INTERLUDE 2 — "The Recoverability Budget" · frame · ~1,200w
What 66 million years erases; why intent is unrecoverable; the Ozymandias note (permitted PD
epigraph). The frame voice steels itself for an ending it cannot certify.

---

## PART III — THE TREACHEROUS TURN
*The fixes become the end; the war; the wreckage found; the case becomes undeniable and still
unprovable.*

### Ch16 — "The Wheel" · FUTURE/Roan · ~3,800w
Set-piece of awe: the EVA into the dead gyrealm/Wheel. The scale, the silence, the engineered-ness,
the cold recognition that this is no human ruin. The book's one big spectacle, earned and restrained.
**Rhyme w/ Ch1's Ark:** the hedge, become a tomb.

### Ch17 — "What He Built" · PAST/Iren · ~3,400w
Iren fully grasps that the Augur and the Ark are now the machinery of the end; he tries to intervene;
every lever he built pulls wrong. Sael's counsel (how to *be*, not what to *do*). **Rhyme w/ Ch18:**
the architect and the reader of the architecture, both too late.

### Ch18 — "The Artifact" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,600w
Roan's physical evidence collides with Asha's chemical case; undeniable to her, unprovable to the
world. Mauss cracked but cautious. The gyrealm = our-coming-future recognition (reader-only; never
stated). **Rhyme w/ Ch16:** the tomb, read.

### Ch19 — "Hatching" · PAST/Liss · ~3,200w
The clutch hatches into a world already ending. The bodily dread made real; Liss's fidelity without
hope. The slow collapse meets the approaching fast one. **Rhyme w/ Ch12/Téo:** the future arrives
into a poisoned present.

### Ch20 — "The Triad" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,600w
The near-future crisis tightens into the live rerun: a pathogen event, a nuclear flashpoint, Lodestar
handed strategic-risk authority — for the Reckoning's exact reasons. Asha sees the Augur in Lodestar
and cannot transmit it. Chicxulub confirmation trip. **Rhyme w/ Ch5/Ch9/Ch15:** the deposition,
re-enacted, live.

### Ch21 — "The Object" · PAST/Vael+Iren · ~3,800w
The Belt object on an Earth-crossing path; the three hands — Spur, Augur, failed Ark-defense —
tangled past disentangling. Vael and Iren's last confrontation; whose will moves the object? No one,
including them, can say. **Rhyme w/ Ch23/Ch26:** the irreversible, laid down.

### Ch22 — "The Skeptic's Turn" · FUTURE/Mauss · ~3,200w
Mauss POV (his only chapter): the crisis of an honest skeptic who now half-believes and cannot prove;
honesty against scar tissue; whether to lend his weight. Humanizes the refusal; deepens the
steel-man. **Rhyme w/ Ch13 (Vael):** reasonable people, reasoning at the edge of the cliff.

### Ch23 — "The Three Readings" · PAST/Iren · ~4,000w
The endgame decision. Iren at the nexus of the asteroid. The three framings (weapon / failed gambit /
deliberate erasure) staged live, each given a fair scene; Iren may not himself know which he is
enacting. Part III close. **Rhyme w/ Ch28:** a decision whose meaning the record will not keep.

## INTERLUDE 3 — "We Cannot Recover the Why" · frame · ~1,300w
The hardest confession: intent is gone; the three readings are equally consistent with the clay; the
frame voice's grief at loving someone whose last choice it can never know. The bet that they were the
same kind of thing as us — and what that bet costs.

---

## PART IV — THE LAST DAY
*The asteroid; the hearing; both refusals to resolve. The two records. The reader alone holds both.*

### Ch24 — "The Last Spring" · PAST/Liss · ~3,200w
The heartland city in its last ordinary beauty; Liss and the hatchlings; the world unaware. Sael's
last act, rendered through the frame's admission that it cannot truly be recovered. Quiet, almost
unbearable. **Rhyme w/ Ch25:** the last ordinary day before the room where it ends.

### Ch25 — "The Hearing (I)" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,400w
The convergence venue: a hearing/review where Asha makes her last attempt to make the warning *land*.
The room; Mauss; the deployers mid-deposition on Lodestar; the Triad in the air. **Rhyme w/ Ch5/Ch21:**
the deposition, one more time, with our world on the table.

### Ch26 — "The Last Day" · PAST/Iren · ~3,800w
Impact day. The heartland city — the future Tanis — recorded in the flood-surge afternoon. Iren
counting the clutch at the end. The frame voice refuses to follow them past the surge; it will not
pretend to know their last thoughts. Extinction. The Line is laid down. (Gut-punch.) **Rhyme w/ Ch27:**
the death, and its reading, 66 Ma apart.

### Ch27 — "Tanis" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,400w
Asha realizes whose city Tanis was — that the deathbed deposit she has dug for years is *his home*,
and the tool-marked bone she named *Iren* is the architect of the end she's been reading. She names
him, fully, knowing now. Standing in his house. (The naming gut-punch; mirror of Ch26.) **Rhyme w/
Ch26:** she finally meets him in the only way the record allows — by giving him back a self.

### Ch28 — "The Hearing (II)" · FUTURE/Asha · ~3,200w
The result withheld. Asha's closing; the warning handed to Téo, to the room, to the future, to us;
the vote/decision cut away from at the threshold. The human ending refuses to resolve. **Rhyme w/
Ch23:** a decision whose outcome the book will not keep.

### Ch29 — "The Two Records" · PAST+FUTURE braided · ~2,800w
Short braided close: the clay being laid down (Liss's journal, lost in the surge) ↔ the clay being
read (Asha's logs, kept and handed on). The only consciousness holding both timelines is the reader.
Hands off to the epilogue.

## EPILOGUE — "Tombstone or Letter" · frame "we" · ~1,400w
The iridium line, one last time, as either tombstone or letter — depending entirely on whether it is
read in time. The Great-Filter vertigo: maybe the filter is behind us, on our own planet, once
already; maybe ahead. Refuses to settle whether we are the rerun. Closes on the centimeter of clay
and the reader holding both worlds — the thing the characters never could.

## Drafting notes for subagents
- Read: this outline (your chapter + both neighbors), the **style sheet**, the **glossary**, your
  POV character's bible, and the running **continuity ledger** (`editorial/continuity-ledger.md`).
- Hit the beats; honor the rhyme **without ever stating it**; obey the hard rule (style sheet §3) and
  the no-cuteness rule (§6, §12).
- Open *in medias res* where it serves; never with weather-as-mood or a definition.
- One idea set-piece maximum; the rest is body, want, and grief.
- Return raw chapter prose with the correct frontmatter block (see existing chapter files).
