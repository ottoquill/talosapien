# Troodontidae

**Type:** science · **Used in:** the real lineage the Talos are extrapolated from · **Related:** [encephalization-quotient.md](encephalization-quotient.md), [feathered-dinosaurs.md](feathered-dinosaurs.md), Talos sapiens

## Summary
Troodontidae is a family of small, lightly built, bird-like maniraptoran theropod dinosaurs with relatively large brains, large forward-set eyes, grasping hands, and a specialized sickle-shaped claw on the second toe. They lie very close to the origin of birds, and several lines of evidence — high relative brain size, keen senses, feathers — make them the most "bird-grade" of the non-avian dinosaurs.

## The science
Troodontids are deinonychosaurian maniraptorans — the broader clade that also contains dromaeosaurids and is the immediate outgroup to (or nested near) Avialae. Among non-avian dinosaurs they show the closest approach to an avian condition in several systems at once. Hopson's foundational survey of relative brain size in archosaurs placed the small theropods later grouped as troodontids at the top of the dinosaurian range, with brain-to-body proportions overlapping the lower edge of the living-bird range [hopson1977]. A recent comparative-neurology review reaffirms that maniraptoran theropods approach avian brain–body ratios and that *Troodon* in particular falls at the lower margin of the overlapping bird/mammal range, implying exceptionally high encephalization for a dinosaur [reiner2023]. (How that ratio is turned into a number is covered on [encephalization-quotient.md](encephalization-quotient.md).)

Skeletally, troodontids are gracile and long-legged, with large orbits indicating large eyes and at least partly forward-facing (overlapping, stereoscopic) visual fields, grasping forelimbs, and the retractable enlarged second-toe claw shared with their dromaeosaurid relatives [vanderreest2017]. The Late Jurassic form *Anchiornis*, referred to Troodontidae, preserves abundant body feathers including long feathers on the arms, legs and feet, directly tying the group to the feathered-dinosaur record [hu2009] (see [feathered-dinosaurs.md](feathered-dinosaurs.md)).

A naming caution: the genus *Troodon* itself is taxonomically unstable. *Troodon formosus* was erected on an isolated tooth, and van der Reest & Currie argued the name is effectively undiagnostic (a *nomen dubium*), resurrecting *Stenonychosaurus* and erecting the new taxon *Latenivenatrix* for well-preserved Dinosaur Park Formation material formerly lumped into *Troodon* [vanderreest2017]. Much older literature (and many museum labels) still says "*Troodon*"; the underlying animals are the same gracile troodontids, but the genus assignment is contested.

## Established vs. open
**Established:** Troodontids are real, well-characterized small maniraptoran theropods with large brains for dinosaurs, large eyes, grasping hands, a sickle claw, feathers, and a close phylogenetic position to birds [hopson1977][reiner2023][hu2009].

**Open / debated:** The validity and content of the genus *Troodon* (vs. *Stenonychosaurus*/*Latenivenatrix*) is actively disputed [vanderreest2017]; the precise position of Troodontidae relative to dromaeosaurids and birds shifts between analyses; and inferences about *behaviour* and *cognition* from brain proportions are proxies, not direct measurements (see [encephalization-quotient.md](encephalization-quotient.md)).

## In *Talosapien*
> The Talos (*Talos sapiens*) are a fictional sapient lineage the novel extrapolates from **real** troodontid biology — the large-brained, large-eyed, feathered, grasping-handed, sickle-clawed body plan described above is not invented. What the book invents is the *outcome*: a troodontid lineage that crosses into language and technology, which no real troodontid did. The page deliberately keeps the science (anatomy, high relative brain size, feathers, bird-kinship) separate from the fiction (sapience). The novel also leans into the genuine *Troodon* taxonomic mess as flavour — its paleontologists argue about names the way real ones do.
