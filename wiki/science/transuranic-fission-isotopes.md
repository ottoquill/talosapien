# Transuranic & Fission-Product Isotopes

**Type:** science · **Used in:** Asha's case; the boundary clay chemistry (the novel's central fictional anomaly) · **Related:** the Line; iridium anomaly; the arsenal

## Summary
Transuranic elements (atomic number Z > 92 — neptunium, plutonium, and heavier) are essentially absent from the natural Earth: aside from vanishing traces produced by neutron capture in uranium ores and a little primordial plutonium-244, they do not occur naturally and are made by human nuclear fission. The same fission also makes characteristic radioactive fission products such as caesium-137, iodine-129, technetium-99, and strontium-90. Because these isotopes are so rare in nature, their appearance in sediment is a powerful anthropogenic marker — indeed the 1945-onward nuclear-fallout signal is a leading candidate marker for the proposed Anthropocene. This is the real science the novel borrows; the appearance of such isotopes in a 66-million-year-old layer is the book's fiction, flagged below.

## The science
Transuranic ("beyond uranium") elements are those with atomic number greater than 92: neptunium (93), plutonium (94), and the heavier actinides. With one near-exception they are not part of Earth's natural inventory, because their half-lives are short compared with the age of the Earth and they were not produced in lasting amounts by the planet's own geology. Trace natural quantities do exist: neptunium-237 and plutonium-239 are continually generated in tiny amounts when neutrons from the spontaneous fission of uranium are captured by uranium nuclei in ores. A primordial survivor — plutonium-244, with a half-life of ~80 million years, just long enough that a remnant from before the Earth formed is conceivable — was reported in 1971 in Precambrian bastnaesite [hoffman1971], but that detection has never been reproduced, and later, far more sensitive accelerator-mass-spectrometry searches found none and set upper limits well below the 1971 value, so the original claim cannot have been a primordial-²⁴⁴Pu signal [lachner2012]. Its natural terrestrial presence is therefore unconfirmed. The net effect only sharpens the picture: even the one transuranic that could plausibly linger from Earth's formation is, at best, vanishingly rare and quite possibly absent, so for practical purposes transuranics are a signature of artificial nuclear processes.

Nuclear fission — whether in reactors or weapons — splits heavy nuclei into lighter "fission products" and releases neutrons that breed transuranics. The fission products include long- and medium-lived radioisotopes such as caesium-137, strontium-90, iodine-129, and technetium-99 that have negligible natural background. Nature has run this process only once on its own: the Oklo deposit in Gabon, where about two billion years ago — when natural uranium was far more enriched in fissile U-235 — uranium ore bodies sustained natural fission "reactors" for hundreds of thousands of years. Oklo is the only known natural fission reactor; it was recognized in 1972 from anomalously depleted U-235 (about 0.717% rather than the normal 0.720%), and the fission products and plutonium it bred remained largely confined within metres of the reaction zones, held in place by surrounding clay and reducing conditions [gauthier-lafaye1996].

Because artificial fission isotopes have essentially no natural background, their global appearance is a sharp time marker. The mid-twentieth-century onset of atmospheric nuclear-weapon testing spread plutonium and fission radionuclides (notably caesium-137 and the bomb-pulse plutonium signal) worldwide as fallout, producing a near-synchronous, globally distributed horizon in sediments, ice, and other archives. The Anthropocene Working Group's synthesis argues that this artificial-radionuclide fallout signal, together with novel materials and other markers, makes the proposed Anthropocene functionally and stratigraphically distinct, with a candidate boundary in the mid-twentieth century [waters2016].

## Established vs. open
**Established:** Transuranics are essentially absent in nature apart from minute neutron-capture traces; the one possible primordial survivor, plutonium-244, was reported in 1971 [hoffman1971] but the result has not been reproduced and later AMS searches found none, leaving its natural occurrence unconfirmed [lachner2012]; fission products and transuranics are produced by nuclear fission, demonstrated naturally only at Oklo two billion years ago [gauthier-lafaye1996]; the post-1945 fallout radionuclide signal is real, globally distributed, and a leading candidate marker for the Anthropocene [waters2016].

**Open / debated:** The formal definition of the Anthropocene — whether it should be ratified as a geological epoch at all, and if so its exact base and primary marker (the fallout signal versus other candidates) — remains contested among stratigraphers [waters2016]. None of this debate concerns deep time: there is no scientific claim of artificial transuranics or fission products in any pre-human layer.

## In *Talosapien*
> Everything above is real science, and the novel keeps it intact. The fiction — the load-bearing speculative claim of the whole book — is that Asha finds transuranic and fission-product isotopes (a plutonium signature, fission products like caesium-137 read through their stable daughters or longer-lived siblings) inside the 66-million-year-old K–Pg boundary clay, where no natural process and no twentieth-century contamination can put them. This is invented. In reality there is no such signal at the K–Pg; any genuine 20th-century fallout transuranics are surface-young, and the short-lived fission products of a hypothetical ancient civilization would have decayed away across 66 million years, leaving only their stable end-products and a handful of very long-lived nuclides — which is precisely the forensic problem the novel makes Asha solve. The book treats this as the one anomaly that cannot be explained by diagenesis or the impact: a smoking gun for a prior industrial civilization (the [Silurian hypothesis](../ideas/silurian-hypothesis.md)), and the reason the [arsenal](../lore/tech/the-arsenal.md) matters. Read alongside the [δ¹³C/δ¹⁵N excursions](d13c-d15n-excursions.md), it is the core of the case Asha builds along [the Line](../lore/glossary/the-line.md). See also [the detection science](../lore/tech/the-detection-science.md).
