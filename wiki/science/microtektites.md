# Microtektites & Impact Spherules

**Type:** science · **Used in:** the Line; the manufactured-spherule conceit · **Related:** shocked quartz, iridium anomaly, Tanis deathbed deposit

## Summary
Impact spherules are tiny glassy or crystalline droplets — typically sub-millimetre to a few millimetres — formed when rock melted or vaporized by an impact is flung into the atmosphere, solidifies, and rains back down. At the K–Pg boundary they occur worldwide: glassy *microtektites* and their devitrified equivalents (*microkrystites*, often with relict crystal textures), concentrated in a thin ejecta layer that thickens toward the Chicxulub crater.

## The science
During a large impact, shock melting and vaporization of target rock produce a spray of molten and condensed material. Droplets quench in flight to glass (microtektites) or, on devitrification or condensation, develop crystalline textures (microkrystites). Their global distribution and the way the ejecta layer thickens toward the source crater make them, like shocked quartz and iridium, a fingerprint of impact.

Smit's review of K–Pg impact-ejecta stratigraphy groups the ejecta into a regional pattern keyed to distance from Chicxulub: thick breccias inside and near the crater rim; complex, tsunami-influenced sequences in the Gulf of Mexico region; an intermediate zone (roughly 2,500–4,000 km out) with tektite- and spherule-bearing layers; and a distal, globe-spanning zone where ejecta appear as a millimetre-thin layer [smit1999]. The consensus review of Schulte et al. likewise treats the spherule/ejecta layer as part of the single, globally distributed Chicxulub signature coincident with the extinction [schulte2010]. At Tanis, ejecta spherules even appear lodged in fish gills, dating their fall to within hours of impact [depalma2019].

## Established vs. open
**Established:** K–Pg impact spherules (microtektites and microkrystites) are real, globally distributed, and thicken toward Chicxulub; they are a standard impact-ejecta indicator [smit1999][schulte2010].

**Open / debated:** Detailed correlation of individual spherule layers between distant sections, the degree of reworking, and arguments by a minority for multiple impact events are still discussed. The impact origin of the spherule layer itself is consensus.

## In *Talosapien*
> Spherules are where the novel's central conceit lives. Real K–Pg spherules are melt droplets — chemically and texturally varied, the messy product of vaporized rock. The book's fictional anomaly is a population of spherules that look *manufactured*: too uniform in size, the wrong glass chemistry, internal structure that reads as engineered rather than quenched. Everything about real microtektites here is accurate; the speculative leap is that a manufactured subset hides among them, a fingerprint a natural impact could not leave.
