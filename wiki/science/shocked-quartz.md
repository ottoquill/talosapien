# Shocked Quartz

**Type:** science · **Used in:** the Line; Asha's case · **Related:** iridium anomaly, microtektites, Chicxulub crater

## Summary
Shocked quartz is quartz that has been deformed by the brief, extreme pressures of a hypervelocity impact, leaving microscopic sets of parallel planar deformation features (PDFs) along specific crystal planes. Such grains are found in the K–Pg boundary clay worldwide and are a diagnostic fingerprint of impact shock — pressures not reached by ordinary volcanism or tectonics.

## The science
When a large bolide strikes, shock waves drive transient pressures of tens of gigapascals through target rock. In quartz this produces multiple sets of straight, closely spaced lamellae — planar deformation features — oriented along rational crystallographic planes, accompanied by reduced refractive index and characteristic streaking in X-ray diffraction. These features are not produced by the lower, slower stresses of volcanic eruptions or normal tectonic strain, which is what makes shocked quartz a reliable impact indicator.

Bohor and colleagues isolated quartz grains with planar features from K–Pg boundary clays at multiple sites in Europe, a north-central Pacific core, and New Zealand; the planar features indexed to rational quartz planes, the grains showed reduced refractive indices, and X-ray photographs showed shock-related streaking [bohor1987]. Because the grains occur at widely separated localities, the authors argued the K–Pg impact distributed shocked ejecta in a globe-encircling dust cloud, supporting the Alvarez impact hypothesis [bohor1987]. The consensus review of Schulte et al. treats shocked quartz, together with iridium and impact spherules, as part of the coherent global ejecta signature of the Chicxulub impact [schulte2010].

## Established vs. open
**Established:** Planar deformation features in quartz are diagnostic of impact-level shock pressures; shocked quartz occurs in K–Pg boundary clays worldwide and is part of the Chicxulub ejecta signature [bohor1987][schulte2010].

**Open / debated:** Fine details such as grain-size gradients with distance from Chicxulub, and the exact mix of source lithologies, are still studied. The impact origin of K–Pg shocked quartz is not in dispute.

## In *Talosapien*
> Shocked quartz is one of the real, unforgeable signatures the novel respects completely — PDFs are physics, and the book never tampers with them. Their role in the story is contrast: shocked quartz is what a *natural* impact undeniably leaves, the honest baseline against which the alleged anomalies (manufactured spherules, transuranic isotopes) are supposed to stand out. When characters argue about what is and isn't impact-derived, shocked quartz is the thing nobody disputes.
