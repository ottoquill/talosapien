# Radiometric Dating

**Type:** science · **Used in:** how the Line is dated; Asha's case · **Related:** K–Pg boundary, geomagnetic polarity stratigraphy, Deccan Traps

## Summary
Radiometric (radioisotopic) dating fixes the absolute age of rocks by measuring the decay of unstable parent isotopes into stable daughters at known rates. Two methods — ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar and U-Pb geochronology — are the workhorses for the K–Pg interval and are the reason the boundary is among the most precisely dated events in deep time, pinned to ~66.0 Ma.

## The science
Radioisotopic clocks rely on the fact that a parent isotope decays to a daughter at a constant, measurable rate; measuring the parent-to-daughter ratio in a closed mineral system yields the time since the mineral formed or last reset. The ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar method (an advanced variant of K-Ar dating) dates potassium-bearing minerals such as sanidine by irradiating a sample to convert ³⁹K to ³⁹Ar, then measuring argon isotope ratios; its accuracy depends on careful intercalibration of neutron-fluence monitor standards against primary standards, the framework for which was laid out by Renne and colleagues [renne1998]. The U-Pb method dates uranium-bearing minerals such as zircon through the decay of ²³⁸U and ²³⁵U to lead isotopes, and is prized for high precision in volcanic ash beds.

For the K–Pg boundary, ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar dating of impact-related tektites and bracketing ash beds placed the boundary at approximately 66.0 Ma and, critically, demonstrated that the Chicxulub impact and the mass extinction were synchronous to within about 32,000 years — a result that hinges on the precision of the method [renne2013]. A later high-resolution study combining ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar dating with magnetostratigraphy in the Hell Creek region returned a K–Pg age near 66.05 Ma and calibrated the surrounding magnetic chron [sprain2018]. The same two methods, applied to the Deccan Traps, produced the competing eruption-tempo results that drive the volcanism debate — itself a demonstration of how much rests on geochronological precision [renne2013][sprain2018].

## Established vs. open
**Established:** ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar and U-Pb dating give reproducible absolute ages; properly intercalibrated, they place the K–Pg boundary at ~66.0 Ma and establish impact–extinction synchrony to within tens of thousands of years [renne1998][renne2013][sprain2018].

**Open / debated:** Small systematic offsets between methods, decay-constant and standard-age refinements, and resulting disagreements at the few-tens-of-thousands-of-years level (e.g., Deccan tempo) are ongoing. The ~66.0 Ma boundary age is robust.

## In *Talosapien*
> Dating is the spine of Asha's argument: nothing in the novel matters unless the anomalies sit *exactly* at the boundary and nowhere else. The book uses real ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar and U-Pb precision as the standard the alleged industrial signals must meet — if a transuranic trace cannot be pinned to the 66.0 Ma horizon to the same resolution that pins Chicxulub there, it doesn't count. The geochronology is unaltered; the fiction is only what the precisely dated layer is claimed to contain.
