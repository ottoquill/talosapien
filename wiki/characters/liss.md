# Liss — character bible

**Talos sapiens.** Field ecologist / atmospheric scientist; Iren's bondmate. The ground-truth of the
deep-past thread: the one who *watches the world die the slow way* before it dies the fast way. Name
**assigned by the reconstruction**.

## One-line
The naturalist who kept the record of a biosphere's last decline, and understood, before anyone, that
they were already dead — they just hadn't been told the date.

## Want / Need / Fear / Wound
- **Want:** to *document* — to keep an honest record of the living world even as it fails, because
  the record is the only fidelity she can offer it.
- **Need:** to be *heard* by Iren and the Concord — to make abstraction-people feel a single dying
  river. (She and Asha share this need across 66 Ma; never stated.)
- **Fear:** that the clutch will hatch into a world already ending; the specific, bodily dread of a
  parent who has read the data.
- **Wound:** she was the first to measure the engineered collapse and was not believed soon enough —
  her own small Cassandra-failure, which is why she stopped arguing and started simply *recording*.

## Body & manner
- Her crest-flush is undisciplined and she lets it be — she is the honest body in a world of held
  ones, which makes her unbearable to politicians and beloved by Iren. Her throat goes grey often
  now, and she does not hide it.
- She keeps a personal record — a naturalist's journal in their medium — of species as they wink
  out. (The reconstruction infers its existence from a habit; the frame voice flags this. It rhymes
  with Asha's core logs and Mara Vance's notebook in *Sentience* — a private keeping-of-what-is-lost.)

## Relationships
- **Iren** — she loves him and is the limit of his abstraction; the argument between *documenting*
  and *engineering* the catastrophe is the argument of their bond. She does not blame him; that is
  worse.
- **the clutch** — her future and her terror, tended in the crèche.

## Arc
- **I/II:** measures the engineered collapse; tries and fails to move the Concord; turns from
  advocacy to record-keeping. Grief becomes method.
- **III:** the war and the Augur's turn make the slow collapse moot; she keeps recording anyway,
  which becomes the book's image of fidelity-without-hope.
- **IV:** she is at the heartland city with the clutch on the last day. The reconstruction can only
  follow her to the flood-surge. The frame voice will not pretend to know her last thought, and says
  so. Her record is, in-world, part of what is *not* recovered — the loss inside the loss.

## Function in the book
Liss is the **heart** of the deep-past thread and the antidote to its abstraction: she keeps the
dying *specific*. She is also the structural twin of Asha — two women keeping honest records of a
death no one will act on in time — a rhyme the prose must never name.
