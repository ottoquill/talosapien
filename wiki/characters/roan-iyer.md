# Roan Iyer — character bible

**Human.** Asteroid-belt salvage-and-survey pilot; secondary POV. The one human who stands *inside*
the physical proof. ~2044.

## One-line
A working pilot who went out to the Belt for a paycheck and found the only large thing the dead
civilization left behind — and could not afford, in every sense, to understand it.

## Want / Need / Fear / Wound
- **Want:** a viable claim; to keep the ship flying and the debts paid; the unromantic want of a
  blue-collar spacer in a frontier just barely industrializing.
- **Need:** to let the find *mean* something — to stop treating the impossible artifact as salvage
  tonnage and reckon with what it is. The arc from "what's it worth" to "what is it."
- **Fear:** vacuum-practical fears (decompression, debt, being stranded) layered over the dawning
  larger one: that the wreck is a mirror.
- **Wound:** lost a crewmate (or a previous claim, or a partner planetside) — a person-sized grief
  that makes the civilization-sized grief legible to the reader through Roan's smaller scale.

## Manner & function
- Laconic, competent, funny in the bleak spacer register; the **body** of the near-future thread the
  way Liss is the body of the deep-past one — sensory, physical, grounded. Roan is how the reader
  *feels* the Belt and touches the gyrealm wreckage; the EVA into the dead Wheel is the book's one
  set-piece of awe.
- Provides the **physical artifact** that Asha's chemical case lacked — the thing that is not
  deniable as terrestrial contamination, and the thing that makes the gyrealm/Wheel identification
  land. The collision of Roan's thread and Asha's is the Part III hinge.

## The gyrealm beat (ties to the gyrealm canon)
Roan and his outfit talk casually about humanity's coming **gyrealms** — the rotating habitats
everyone expects to build this century. When Roan finally understands that the ancient wreck *is* a
gyrealm — that the ancients built the exact thing we are about to — the recognition is the book's
quietest, largest chord, and it is left to the reader; Roan only feels the cold of it.

## Arc
- **I/II (backgrounded):** working the survey; the anomalous orbit and the too-old debris he keeps
  meaning to write up.
- **III:** the EVA; the identification; contact with Asha; the artifact changes both their cases and
  both their lives.
- **IV:** Roan carries the physical evidence into the endgame; his plain testimony is, in its way,
  more dangerous to Mauss's position than all of Asha's isotopes — and still maybe not enough.
