# Téo Marchetti — character bible

**Human.** Asha Rendel's graduate student; the reader's way in, and the future the warning is
actually *for*. ~2044, mid-twenties.

## One-line
The next generation, who will inherit whichever answer the book declines to give.

## Want / Need / Fear / Wound
- **Want:** to do real science with Asha; ambition braided with genuine awe; a career and a meaning.
- **Need:** to decide what he owes a truth that could end his career before it starts — to choose,
  as a young person, whether to carry Asha's fire or play it safe. His small version of the book's
  big choice.
- **Fear:** that hitching to Asha's Line will sink him; the ordinary, sympathetic careerist fear that
  makes him the reader's realistic proxy (most of us would hesitate).
- **Wound:** lighter than the leads' by design — he is the one with the most future and the least
  scar; that is his narrative job.

## Function
- **Exposition without lecture:** Téo can be *taught* on the page, so the reader learns the science
  through his questions — but he is sharp, so it never feels like hand-holding (style sheet §5).
- **The stakes made personal and forward-facing:** Liss has the clutch (the future as offspring);
  Asha has Téo (the future as inheritor). He is the human answer to *what we owe the people who don't
  exist yet*.
- **The ending's fulcrum:** in Part IV, what Téo decides to do — keep the record, carry it forward,
  or let it go — is part of what the book leaves unresolved. He is, in a sense, the reader, deciding.

## Arc
- **I/II:** student and helper; skeptical-then-converted; the human scale of wonder at the Line.
- **III:** the cost arrives — his own standing is now entangled with Asha's; he must choose.
- **IV:** his choice is left as one of the open hinges; the epilogue's "we" is, partly, the future
  Téos who did or did not keep reading the clay.
