# The Cretaceous World — deep-past setting

The stage of the Talos civilization: Earth in the **late Maastrichtian**, the final ~2 million
years before the K–Pg boundary (~66.0 Ma). Real paleogeography, real climate, real co-stressors —
the Talos crisis plays out against a planet *already* under strain, which is half the tragedy.

## Geography & climate (real)

- **Continents** roughly modern in arrangement but closer: a high **sea level** (broad epicontinental
  seas, including the receding Western Interior Seaway over North America), warm **poles**, no polar
  ice. A hothouse world, forested to high latitudes.
- **Atmosphere:** higher CO₂ than today; the Talos industrial era pushes it further — a fact Liss
  measures and the Concord argues about, exactly as we do. The δ13C record will carry the signature.
- **The Deccan Traps** are *already erupting* — one of the largest volcanic provinces in Earth
  history, a real K–Pg co-stressor. The Talos sited industry and launch in the region (the
  **Deccan works**) precisely because it was geologically active, energy-rich, and — fatally —
  because volcanism would later be the natural alibi for everything they did to the air and the
  isotopes. The rock blames the volcano. That is part of why we almost never found them.

## The biosphere (Liss's domain)

- A world of non-avian dinosaurs in their last flourishing, early flowering plants, insects,
  mammals small and waiting. The Talos are apex *minds* in an ecosystem that does not know it.
- **The engineered collapse:** before the asteroid, the biosphere is already failing — an
  engineered pathogen (a weapon, or a weapon's escape) is moving through key species; pollinator and
  prey-base crashes ripple upward. Liss documents this. The reader should feel the world dying *the
  slow way* before it dies the fast way. (This matters: the asteroid did not interrupt a healthy
  world. It put a healthy-looking world out of an already-mortal misery — another underdetermination
  the humans can never fully resolve in the record.)

## The heartland city (the deathbed)

- The Talos heartland metropolis sits, in modern terms, where **Tanis** (Hell Creek Formation) now
  lies — a river-delta city on the margin of the inland sea. Dense, vertical-but-low, built for
  bodies that fly-fall and balance; warm crèche-districts at the protected core.
- It is where Iren and Liss keep their clutch. It is where, 66 million years later, Asha Rendel digs
  — and where the deathbed deposit preserves, in a single day's flood-surge, the last hours.
- **The poignancy engine:** Tanis is a real site famous for preserving the *day of impact*. In the
  novel, it preserves the day of impact *of a city*. Asha is standing in Iren's home and does not
  know it for most of the book.

## Space — the Wheels in the Belt

- The Talos crossed cislunar space and built **rotating habitats** ("the Wheels") in the **asteroid
  belt**, where raw material is abundant and a catastrophe on Earth would not reach. Engineering
  identical in principle to the human **gyrealm** concept (O'Neill cylinders): spun for gravity,
  pressurized, biome'd. The Ark program (Iren's) intended them as a hedge for the species.
- They became, instead, a strategic high ground, a place the Spur could stage from, and — in one of
  the three readings of the end — the launch infrastructure for redirecting a Belt object at Earth.
- Their wreckage still rides Belt orbits 66 million years on: anomalous, ancient, engineered debris
  with isotope signatures no natural body carries. This is what Roan Iyer finds. (The humans call
  them gyrealms before they understand. See the gyrealm worldbuilding canon for the habitat
  engineering this borrows.)

## What survives to be read (the recoverability budget)

Per the Silurian Hypothesis, almost nothing skeletal or built survives 66 Ma of subduction, erosion,
and sedimentation. What does, and what the novel is allowed to hang evidence on:
- **The Line:** a global iridium anomaly (real, Alvarez 1980) — but with *additional*, in-world
  anomalies the natural impact cannot explain: **transuranic and fission-product isotopes**,
  industrial **δ15N**, trace-metal layering, and microstructure in spherules.
- **Tool-marked bone** and a few mineralized fragments at the deathbed deposit.
- **The Belt wreckage** — the only large physical artifacts, preserved because space erodes slowly.
- **Nothing legible.** No text, no image, no name, no face. The hard floor of the reconstruction.
