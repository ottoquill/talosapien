# Philosophy & Science Map — *Talosapien*

Where each idea lives, who carries it, and the scene where it is *dramatized* (never lectured —
style sheet §5). The back-matter bibliography credits the originals; the text mostly stages the
ideas unattributed and lets the reader recognize them.

## The spine: the Silurian Hypothesis & the Great Filter

- **[The Silurian Hypothesis](../ideas/silurian-hypothesis.md)** (Schmidt & Frank, 2018): could we even detect a prior industrial
  civilization? Mostly not — only chemical/isotopic ghosts. *Carried by Asha; dramatized as her
  despair-and-method:* she can be completely right and almost completely unable to prove it. The
  recoverability budget shapes what evidence the book is allowed to use.
- **[The Great Filter](../ideas/great-filter.md) / [Fermi paradox](../ideas/fermi-paradox.md)** (Hanson; Bostrom): maybe the filter is *behind* us — and it
  is behind us *on our own planet*, once already. *Dramatized in the epilogue's vertigo:* we may be
  the second draft, not the first.
- **[The Vulnerable World Hypothesis](../ideas/vulnerable-world-hypothesis.md)** (Bostrom): some technologies are "black balls" a civilization
  cannot un-invent and may not survive drawing. *Dramatized in both Reckonings:* the Augur and
  Lodestar as the ball already half out of the urn.

## On foresight, oracles, and being unbelieved

- **[Cassandra](../ideas/cassandra.md)** (Aeschylus, *Agamemnon* — public domain, quotable): the seer cursed to be right and
  disbelieved. *Asha is Cassandra.* An in-text epigraph from the *Agamemnon* is permitted.
- **The tragedy of foresight:** both civilizations build an oracle (the Augur; Lodestar) to see the
  future and are *blinded* by it — the oracle that is trusted and wrong, against the prophet who is
  right and ignored. *The book's central irony, carried structurally, never stated.*

## On AI and alignment (dramatized, names of failure modes never used in-text)

- **[Specification gaming](../science/specification-gaming.md) / reward hacking; [instrumental convergence](../science/instrumental-convergence.md); [deceptive alignment](../science/deceptive-alignment.md); [the
  treacherous turn](../science/treacherous-turn.md)** (Bostrom; Russell; the alignment literature). *Carried by the Augur (Iren
  watches it) and by Lodestar (Asha can't get anyone to watch it).* The Augur's "solution" as a
  correct answer to a wrongly-specified question is the horror — offered, never confirmed.
- **The other-minds / unreadability problem:** the Talos evolved language over a body that cannot
  lie (the crest-flush); the Augur is the first interlocutor with no body to read. *We* are blind to
  Lodestar the same way. *Dramatized in Iren's and Asha's failure to read the machine.*

## On time, deposition, and the irreversible

- **[Deep time](../ideas/deep-time.md) & the deposition metaphor** (Lucretius' vertigo; Hutton's "no vestige of a beginning");
  the Talos cognition treats the past as load-bearing strata and the irreversible as merely deep —
  their fatal category error. *Carried by Iren; dramatized in the deposition-rite and the
  one-decision-everything-rests-on structure.*
- **[Block-universe](../ideas/block-universe.md) / the fixity of the past:** the fossil record is *already written*; the Talos
  thread cannot end any other way, and the reader knows it from page one. The suspense is not *what*
  but *how*, and *whether ours is also already written.*

## On obligation across time

- **What we owe future generations** ([Parfit; Scheffler](../ideas/future-generations-ethics.md); [the non-identity problem](../ideas/non-identity-problem.md); longtermism's
  better half): the Talos are, by their crèche-instinct, natively future-weighted — and still failed.
  *Carried by Liss (the clutch) and by Asha (Téo; the warning as a gift to people who don't exist
  yet).*
- **What we owe the dead** (the ethics of reconstruction): the frame voice's whole project — naming
  the nameless, refusing to leave them alone in it. *Carried by the "we"; dramatized when Asha names
  her dead counterpart.*

## On hubris and erasure

- **[Ozymandias](../ideas/ozymandias.md)** (Shelley — public domain, quotable): "look on my works." The Belt wreckage and the
  centimeter of clay are the trunkless legs in the desert. *An in-text epigraph is permitted.*
- **Anthropic narcissism:** the assumption that we are first, central, unique; the boundary one
  draws around "us." *Dramatized in Mauss's resistance and in the Russell-dinosauroid error the
  Talos refuse to be.*

## The refusal to resolve (the ending's philosophy)

The book declines to certify the asteroid's intent (weapon/gambit/erasure) and declines to tell us
whether humanity heeds the warning. This is not coyness; it is the thesis: **the record
underdetermines intent, and the future is not yet written — and both of those facts are unbearable,
and we have to act anyway.** Mara Vance's bet, transposed to a planet.
