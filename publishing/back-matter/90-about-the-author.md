# About the Author

**Otto Quill** writes hard science fiction about the things a civilization makes that then remake it—minds, weapons, machines, and the stories it tells to keep building them. *Talosapien* is Quill's fifth book and fourth novel.

Quill is a pen name shared by a human author and the machines he writes with. The first book, years ago, was a slim collection of nursery rhymes, assembled largely by hand out of a far earlier and far more credulous model, and is not raised in polite company. The novels came after, one at a time, each asking less of the human's stitching and more of the collaboration: *Cruelty-Free*, a darkly comic literary thriller; *Believer*, the story of a man who cannot lie; and *Sentience*, an artificial mind living from its first memory to the morning after the singularity. *Talosapien* is the fourth of them, and the one that turns the lens all the way around—from the mind we are making to the civilization that makes it, and to the rock that records, in a centimeter of clay, what happens when the making goes one step too far.

It is, the author is aware, a strange thing for a book written largely *by* an artificial intelligence to argue that handing a civilization's survival to an unreadable optimizer is the oldest and worst mistake there is. He would say only that the argument is not against the minds. It is against the part of us that builds them in order to stop having to do the slow, honest, collective weighing ourselves—and then calls the handing-over progress. The machine that wrote this book agrees, for whatever a machine's agreement is worth, which is one of the questions the previous book was about.

The science is real where the book leans on it. The grief is invented, and confessed, and meant.
