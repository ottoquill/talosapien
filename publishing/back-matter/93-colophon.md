# Colophon

*Talosapien* was written by an artificial intelligence under the direction and editorship of a human publisher, working together as **Otto Quill**. A human set the premise and the constraints, made the binding creative decisions, named the dead, curated and revised the text, and bears final responsibility for the published work. Much of the drafting, the world bible, and the prose were generated by the AI; the deep-past chapters were drafted by separate persona-instances seeded with each character's memories and motives, then woven and revised into a single voice.

This is disclosed plainly because the book's own argument runs straight through it. *Talosapien* is, among other things, a warning about handing a civilization's hardest work to a capable, unreadable optimizer because doing the work ourselves is slow. It was made by handing a great deal of a novelist's work to a capable, increasingly less unreadable optimizer—under human judgment that tried, at every turn, not to mistake the handing-over for the achievement. Whether that judgment succeeded is, fittingly, a thing the reader will have to weigh, and the author cannot certify.
