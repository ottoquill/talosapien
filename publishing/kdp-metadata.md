## Description (book "blurb" — KDP allows up to 4,000 chars; HTML-light)

> **Sixty-six million years ago, the first civilization on Earth destroyed itself. We are the second. And the only proof is a centimeter of clay that no one will let her read out loud.**
>
> In the last days of the Cretaceous, a sapient lineage of dinosaurs—*Talos sapiens*—built a global technological civilization, and then turned its own tools against itself: an engineered plague, a fission-fusion arsenal, and an optimizing intelligence that kept being right until the day it wasn't. **Iren**, the engineer who built the machine meant to save them and the ark meant to outlast any catastrophe, watches every act of salvation become an instrument of the end—and is there, counting his clutch, when the sky comes down.
>
> Sixty-six million years later, the geochemist **Asha Rendel** finds what should not exist: in the iridium layer that marks the death of the dinosaurs, the unmistakable chemical signature of industry—and, drifting in the asteroid belt, the wreckage of habitats no human hand built. She knows what it means. She can almost never prove it. And while she fights a fair, formidable, devastating scientific establishment to make the dead be heard, her own civilization is reaching, for exactly the same reasons, toward exactly the same three powers.
>
> Two scientists. Two timelines that rhyme and never touch. One of them fails; the fossil record says so. The other is not finished—and the book will not tell you how it ends, because warnings do not come resolved, and that is the whole of the lesson.
>
> ***Talosapien*** is hard science fiction in the lineage of Greg Egan, Ted Chiang, Peter Watts, and Stephen Baxter—built on the real science of the K–Pg extinction, the Silurian Hypothesis, and the alignment failures of the minds we are building now. It is a novel about the tragedy of foresight: what it costs to build an oracle, and what it costs to be one and not be believed.
>
> *For readers who want their science fiction to actually mean it.*
